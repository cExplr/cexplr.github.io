A Case Study of an Incorrect “bitwise and” Optimization in V8

6 April 2022

Earlier this month, I have the honor of presenting a small talk for NUS Security Wednesday. The main goal was to do a small case study on CVE-2021-30599, a bug reported by @manfp in Google Chrome V8 Javascript Engine. The report was really well written and so unlike my previous analysis, I aimed to “reverse engineer” the author’s thought process during the exploit development. The reason is, the bug found was seemingly harmless but @manfp managed to transform that to a type-confusion bug, leading to Out Of Bounds access, and chained that with a typer hardening bypass in Chrome V8.

This presentation is an introduction to developing a proof-of-concept for a Chrome V8 Turbofan vulnerability. In this session, we will take a look at an example, starting with what the bug is, and how the author of an incorrect optimization bug might have developed the POC leading to an out-of-bounds access. Details of exploitation will not be covered but an overview will be given.
